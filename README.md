# International Student Mental Health Analysis

This project analyzes mental health among international and domestic students at a Japanese university, focusing on the impact of social connectedness, acculturative stress, and length of stay.

## Data

The dataset includes:
- Student type (international or domestic)
- Language proficiency (Japanese and English)
- Academic level
- Age
- Length of stay
- Depression score (PHQ-9)
- Social connectedness score (SCS)
- Acculturative stress score (ASISS)

## Analysis

- SQL queries were used to explore group differences and trends.
- Key metrics: average depression, social connectedness, and acculturative stress by length of stay.

## Key Findings

- International students show higher risk of mental health difficulties.
- Social connectedness and acculturative stress are predictive of depression.
- Length of stay may influence these outcomes.
